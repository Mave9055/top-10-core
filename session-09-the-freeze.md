## SESSION 09: THE FREEZE

**Time:** 75 minutes  
**Law:** You stop when you can't fight or run.  
**Tool:** *"This is freeze. My body is trying to save me. I am safe now."*

---

### [0:00 – 10:00] THE ANCHOR

(Standard grounding exercise: Feet flat, hand on chest, 4-count breath.)

Last week we talked about how you act (War-Work and Disappearing). Today we talk about why you *don't* act.

**Law 9: The Freeze.**

---

### [10:00 – 40:00] THE MECHANISM

When the threat is too big to fight and too fast to outrun, the nervous system has one final biological move: **The Freeze.**

It's not a choice. It's an involuntary biological shutdown. Your heart rate drops, your muscles go limp or rigid, and your brain goes offline.

In the wild, this is "playing dead." It's the last-ditch effort to survive a predator. In the modern world, it looks like:
- Not being able to speak when someone yells at you.
- Feeling "stuck" or "paralyzed" when you have a deadline.
- "Noncompliance" in the legal or medical system.

---

### [40:00 – 60:00] THE SHAME OF THE FREEZE

Society hates the freeze. They call it weakness, laziness, or cowardice.

But the freeze is actually the highest level of survival. It means you survived something so overwhelming that your body had to shut down to keep you alive.

---

### [60:00 – 75:00] THE RESET

When you feel the freeze coming on:
1. Acknowledge it: "This is a survival response."
2. Small movements: Wiggle your toes. Tap a finger.
3. Gentle warmth: A warm cup of coffee, a blanket.

You aren't broken. You're just in a protective state.

---
