## WEEK 4: HOW YOU ACT

**Time:** 120 minutes  

---

### [0:00 – 10:00] THE ANCHOR
Feet flat. Hand on chest. Breathe. 

Last week: The Mourning and The Exit. Today: How you survive through overworking and erasing yourself.

---

### [10:00 – 40:00] LAW 5: THE WAR-WORK
**Law:** Work becomes the war you can win.  
**Tool:** *"This isn't discipline. This is panic wearing a suit."*

You're the first one in and the last one out. Everyone calls you a hero. But the moment you stop, the noise comes back.

**The Episode:**
> *"Work became the next addiction... I worked like five people... I got clean. I didn't get free."*
> *"The pattern never changed: Get the job. Overperform. Get praised. Get overwhelmed. Crack. Disappear. Repeat."*

**The Mechanism:**
1. **Control is Safety**: Work is the one place where there are rules and you can feel competent.
2. **Obsession Dressed as Discipline**: You're not winning; you're just fighting a different war to avoid the one inside.

---

### [40:00 – 70:00] LAW 7: THE DISAPPEARING
**Law:** You learned to disappear.  
**Tool:** *"That's the fawn. That's threat mitigation."*

You say yes when you want to scream no. You apologize for being hurt. This is **fawning**.

**The Episode:**
> *"I hid in closets – literally becoming invisible... Proof that my existence felt like something that needed to be hidden."*
> *"I couldn't say no... to someone who didn't even supervise me. Because saying no would mean risking disapproval."*

**The Mechanism:**
1. **Erase Your Needs**: You learned that having a self got you hurt. The safest thing is to be a chameleon.
2. **Threat Mitigation**: Fawning isn't people-pleasing. It's trying to keep the threat calm so you stay alive.

---

- If you've ever physically hidden because being seen felt too dangerous.
