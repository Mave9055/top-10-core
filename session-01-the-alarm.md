## SESSION 01: THE ALARM (BEEFIER VERSION)

**Time:** 75 minutes  
**Law:** Your alarm never shuts off.  
**Tool:** *"My alarm is going off. That's not weakness. That's my body doing its job."*

---

### [0:00 – 8:00] THE ANCHOR

Listen up.

Yellow light. Red light. You run this room. If you need to leave, you leave. If you need to stand, you stand. You are in charge.

Feet flat on the floor. Hand on chest. Hand on belly. Breathe in for four. Hold. Out for six. Do it again. Good.

Last session we made the deal: you own your story, we look at the map. No therapy. No sharing. Just the machine.

Today we learn Law 1. Here it is:

**Your alarm never shuts off.**

You know the feeling. You're sitting in a quiet room. Nothing's wrong. But your heart is pounding. Your jaw is tight. You're scanning for exits. A car backfires and your body hits 140 BPM before your mind even knows what happened.

That's not weakness. That's wiring. Let's see how that works.

---

### [8:00 – 45:00] THE EPISODE (Expanded)

We're going to read something. It's about a man who spent forty-three years thinking he was broken.

(Open the book. Read from Preface, page 2.)

> *"For years, I thought I was broken. Lazy. Toxic. Crazy. My ex-wife told me I was bipolar. Doctors slapped labels on me like tags on a carcass. I believed them. I took the pills. I drank the booze. I smoked the meth. I thought it was a character flaw."*

That's the shame talking. The shame that comes after the alarm goes off for the thousandth time. But listen to what happened next.

> *"Then a therapist looked me in the eye and said, 'It's not what's wrong with you. It's what happened to you.' Complex PTSD. It clicked. Like a key in a rusted lock."*

That key? That's what we're turning today.

Now let's go back to where the alarm got wired. Before he had words for it.

(Read from Chapter 1, page 3-4.)

> *"They said I was born early, but I've always felt late. Three pounds, four ounces of undercooked soul dropped into a box of glass and wires. I spent my first thirty days in an incubator – no mother's arms, no skin-to-skin, just machines and a sterile kind of silence. My body learned distance before my mind even knew the word."*

Let that sink in. **His body learned distance before his mind knew the word.** That's Law 1. The alarm doesn't start with a thought. It starts with a nervous system that got programmed before you could remember.

> *"I didn't know it then, but that box became my blueprint. Don't cry too loud. Don't take up too much space. Stay still and maybe you'll survive."*

Three instructions. Don't cry. Don't take space. Stay still. That's the program. And it runs in the background of every quiet moment, every safe room, every Tuesday afternoon when nothing is wrong.

Now here's where the alarm got reinforced.

(Read from Chapter 1, page 4 – blanket night.)

> *"I was six, huddled under a thin blanket that smelled of mildew and sweat, twisting it in my fists until my knuckles throbbed. My heart pounded like a trapped bird, each beat whispering, It's happening again."*

> *"I wanted to run to her, shield her somehow, but fear was a vise – cold, tight, locking me in place. The same total freeze that would hijack my body decades later. If I moved, I'd be next."*

**"If I moved, I'd be next."** That's the calculation. That's the alarm making a life-or-death decision in a fraction of a second. And it worked. He stayed still. He survived. So the nervous system wrote that down as the rule: *When the threat is big, freeze. Do not move. Do not make noise. Do not exist.*

Now here's the cost of that wiring.

(Read from Interlude, page 5.)

> *"Sleep paralysis is when your brain wakes up before your body does. For people with complex trauma, this hits harder. Childhood trauma wires the nervous system for hypervigilance. The threat detector stays set to sensitive, always ready to blare. Sleep gets broken, and the body never fully relaxes."*

**"The body never fully relaxes."** That's the alarm running 24/7. Even when you're unconscious. Even when you're safe.

(Read the next part – the screams.)

> *"As I grew older, paralysis gave way to something worse: the screams. Not conscious cries, but sounds ripped from somewhere deeper, primal alarms tearing through the night. I'd wake to shaken shoulders, to voices telling me I had been screaming bloody murder, but I remembered nothing."*

Let that land. His body was screaming. His mind had no memory of it. That's not craziness. That's a nervous system so locked in survival mode that it bypassed consciousness entirely. The alarm doesn't care if you're awake. It doesn't care if you're embarrassed. It cares about one thing: keeping you alive.

Now let's look at the blueprint behind all of this.

(Read from Chapter 10, page 22 – Glass Box Blueprint, simplified.)

> *"Think of being born VLBW in that 80s NICU as starting life on shaky ground. When the big storms hit – trauma, loss, toxic relationships – they rocked my foundation harder because it wasn't solid to begin with."*

That's the key. Most people think trauma responses are just about the bad things that happened. But the bad things hit harder when the foundation was already cracked. Your alarm didn't start at zero. It started at a baseline of danger.

(Read from Chapter 12 – 60 Reflections, page 25-26, select a few lines.)

> *"The brain doesn't store trauma as memory. It stores it as threat protocol."*

> *"You can't outperform a dysregulated nervous system. You just exhaust it faster."*

> *"I didn't need more motivation. I needed a nervous system that didn't treat a Tuesday like a threat."*

That's the whole thing. You've been trying to motivate your way out of a biological program. You can't. You have to see the program first. Then you can start to rewrite it.

---

### [45:00 – 55:00] THE MECHANISM (Whiteboard Drawing)

(Walk to whiteboard. Draw large, simple arrows. Use only these elements.)

Step 1: **Early Danger** – NICU, violence, chaos. The body learns: danger is normal.

Step 2: **Blueprint Installed** – "Don't cry. Don't take space. Stay still."

Step 3: **Alarm Soldered to ON** – Threat detector set to max. Never turns off.

Step 4: **Body Dumps Adrenaline** – Heart races. Jaw clenches. Hands cold. You scan.

Step 5: **Shame Hits** – "Why can't I just relax? What's wrong with me?"

Step 6: **Loop Back** – Shame makes the alarm worse. Now you're afraid of being afraid.

Point to each step. Say:

"You're not broken. You're running an old program from a war zone that doesn't exist anymore. The alarm isn't the enemy. It's a soldier that never got the stand-down order."

---

### [55:00 – 60:00] TACTICAL RESET #1

Stop. Feet flat on the floor. Hand on chest. Breathe in for four. Hold. Out for six.

Find three things you can see that are blue. Good. Your brain just checked the perimeter. That's the alarm doing its job. Now you name it. Not fight it. Name it.

Reset complete.

---

### [60:00 – 68:00] THE MIRROR (Silent – No talking)

You don't have to say a word. Just sit. Breathe. And notice.

If you've ever been called "too sensitive."

If you've ever jumped at a loud noise and felt stupid after.

If you've ever scanned a restaurant for exits before you looked at the menu.

If you've ever woken up more tired than when you went to bed.

If you've ever been told "just relax" and wanted to punch the person who said it.

If you've ever felt ashamed because your body reacted to nothing.

If you've ever felt like your brain is a fire alarm that won't shut off.

That's not weakness. That's wiring. That's your body doing exactly what it was trained to do – in a world that no longer requires it.

---

### [68:00 – 75:00] THE SHIFT + CLIFFHANGER

Here's your tool. One sentence.

The next time the alarm goes off – the heart racing, the jaw clenching, the scan for exits – don't fight it. Don't call yourself crazy.

Say it out loud or in your head:

**"My alarm is going off. That's not weakness. That's my body doing its job."**

That's it. You're not trying to turn it off. You're just naming it. Naming it gives your brain one second of air. One second to notice that you're not under a blanket anymore. One second to remember the war is over.

**Next session: Law 2. The Ghosts.** Why you carry weight you didn't earn. Why calm feels dangerous. Why you feel like the other shoe is always about to drop – and it might not even be yours.

Feet flat. Hand on chest. One breath.

You're free. See you next session.
