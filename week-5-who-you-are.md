### [10:00 – 40:00] LAW 6: THE MIRROR
**Law:** You were looking in a broken mirror.  
**Tool:** *"That's not the truth. That's the broken mirror talking."*

Your first attachment is the mirror where you learn who you are. If that mirror is broken, you learn: *I am unlovable. My existence is a problem.*

**The Episode:**
> *"The mirror lies when shame's in the room. It shows you a monster instead of a mechanic."*
> *"Under all that mess, she said, I didn't have a broken personality. I had a trauma injury."*

**The Mechanism:**
1. **The Mother Wound**: If the first mirror can't show you that you matter, you learn you are the problem.
2. **Monster vs. Mechanic**: Shifting from a shame-based identity to a trauma-informed one.

### [40:00 – 70:00] LAW 10: THE CIRCUIT BREAKER
**Law:** You leave your body.  
**Tool:** *"That's the circuit breaker. My system is protecting me. I can come back slowly."*

You're watching from the ceiling. You don't remember the last ten miles. This is **dissociation**.

**The Episode:**
> *"His body was screaming... His mind had no memory of it... The circuit breaker tripped so hard that the experience didn't even get recorded."*
> *"You don't lose your mind. You lose your baseline."*

**The Mechanism:**
1. **The Circuit Breaker Trips**: When the current (pain) is too high, the system throws a switch to keep you from shattering.
2. **Archives, Not Memories**: The brain protects itself by not recording overwhelming input.
