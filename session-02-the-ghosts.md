## SESSION 02: THE GHOSTS

**Time:** 75 minutes  
**Law:** You're carrying ghosts you never met.  
**Tool:** *"That's not mine. That's a ghost."*

---

### [0:00 – 8:00] THE ANCHOR

Listen up.

Yellow light. Red light. You run this room. If you need to leave, you leave. If you need to stand, you stand. You are in charge.

Feet flat on the floor. Hand on chest. Hand on belly. Breathe in for four. Hold. Out for six. Do it again. Good.

Last session: Law 1 – Your alarm never shuts off.

Today: Law 2. Here it is:

**You're carrying ghosts you never met.**

Some of what you feel – the dread, the foreboding, the feeling that something bad is about to happen – some of that isn't even yours. It was passed down to you. From your parents. From your grandparents. From people who died before you were born.

Their trauma wrote itself into your nervous system before you took your first breath. Let's see how that works.

---

### [8:00 – 48:00] THE EPISODE (Expanded)

We're going to read something. It's about a man whose mother, in her dying moment, reached for a ghost.

(Open the book. Read from Chapter 5, page 16.)

> *"The living room filled with paramedics. 'Who am I?' my sister asked. 'Paula,' Mom said – naming her dead sister."*

Let that land.

Her sister Paula was dead. Had been dead for years. But in that room, with the paramedics and the chaos and her own life slipping away, that ghost was more real than her living daughter.

That's Law 2. The dead don't leave. They live in the nervous system. They whisper through your mother's mouth when she's too tired to keep the walls up.

Now read this. (From Chapter 18, page 37 – the list of names. Read slowly, not theatrical. Just naming.)

> *"Sylvie Dean Kraus (1988) – Nicholas D. Williams (1999) – Thomas Paul Williams (2000) – Herbert William Lingar (2001) – Judy Ann Lingar (2003) – Argie Faye Williams (2004) – Ashley Nicole Williams (2005) – Warren Wade Ellett (2007) – Chad Argus Acord (2011) – William Garrett Lingar (2011) – Daniel Lee Stewart (2011) – Jerry Don Williams (2012) – Justin Dean Phillips (2015) – Todd Anthony Lingar (2015) – Marie Elizabeth Lingar (2017) – Gabriel Allen Woods (2018) – Jason Jones (2019) – Paula Faye Pitts (2020) – John Wesley Robinson (2020) – Patricia Lynn Lingar (2021) – Deanna Lynn Teague (2021) – Clint Lee Larrison (2022) – Joseph David Middleton (2024) – Billy Aaron Dean Woods (2025)."*

(Close the book. Pause.)

Twenty-three names. Twenty-three people who died. Some of them he never even met. But their loss lived in his body. Every funeral he couldn't attend. Every goodbye he never got to say. Every piece of grief that got handed down like an heirloom nobody asked for.

Now let's look at what the research says – but I'm not going to use those fancy words. Here's what it means in plain talk.

(Read from Chapter 10, page 22 – simplified.)

> *"Research suggests VLBW folks face higher risks: anxiety, depression, attachment issues, sensory hypersensitivity, altered stress response. My life has been a highlight reel of trauma hitting these exact vulnerabilities."*

But here's the part that matters. You didn't just inherit your parents' eye color. You inherited their alarm settings.

(Read from Chapter 10, page 23 – the feedback loop.)

> *"It became a feedback loop. The VLBW vulnerabilities got dialed up by later traumas. Early stress likely primed me for hyperarousal, making childhood terror hit harder, digging the trenches for Complex PTSD deeper."*

Let me translate. Your grandparents survived something. War. Poverty. Violence. A house where love was a weapon. Their bodies learned: *The world is not safe. Never let your guard down.*

They had kids. Your parents. Those kids were born into that nervous system. Not just the behavior. The actual biological settings. High alert. Fast startle. Always scanning.

Then your parents lived their own storms. Maybe they passed it on again. Maybe they made it worse.

Then you were born. And you didn't start at zero. You started with their war already inside you.

Now listen to how this feels from the inside.

(Read from Prologue, page 3.)

> *"People call it paranoia. I call it data. My brain predicts the worst because the worst is usually what happens. The Start: Born dying in an incubator. Wiring set to danger. The Systems: Cops ruled my cousin's death suicide when it was murder. So yeah, I don't trust the legal system. The Loss: When you lose twenty- three people, expecting tragedy isn't negative. It's realistic."*

That's Law 2. Your brain isn't broken. It's pattern-recognizing from a life – and a family history – where the worst kept happening.

One more piece. This is from the 60 Reflections. (Read from Chapter 12, page 26.)

> *"The body keeps score. The mind keeps receipts. The soul keeps the tab."*

Every loss adds to the tab. Every death. Every betrayal. Every goodbye you never got to say. The body doesn't forget. The body keeps score. And when the tab gets too high, your baseline shifts. You start expecting the worst. Because the worst is what you've seen.

That's why calm feels suspicious. That's why you're always waiting for the next bad thing.

You're not broken. You're the latest carrier of a long line of survival programs.

---

### [48:00 – 58:00] THE MECHANISM (Whiteboard Drawing)

(Walk to whiteboard. Draw large, simple arrows.)

Here's how the ghosts get passed down. Watch.

**Step 1:** Draw a box. Label it **"GRANDPARENTS' WAR"**

Before you were born, your grandparents survived something terrible. War. Violence. Poverty. Their nervous systems learned: Danger is normal. Never relax.

**Step 2:** Draw an arrow to a second box. Label it **"THEIR BODY CHANGES"**

Their alarms got soldered on. Just like Law 1. High cortisol. Fast startle. Hypervigilance. This isn't psychology. It's biology.

**Step 3:** Draw an arrow to a third box. Label it **"THEY HAVE KIDS"**

Your parents are born. They don't just get eye color and height. They get those alarm settings too. It's in the wiring.

**Step 4:** Draw an arrow to a fourth box. Label it **"YOUR PARENTS' OWN TRAUMA"**

Then your parents live their own storms. Maybe they repeat the violence. Maybe they freeze. Maybe they run. The alarm settings get reinforced. Sometimes they get worse.

**Step 5:** Draw an arrow to a fifth box. Label it **"YOU ARE BORN"**

You come out already wired for a war you never fought. Before the NICU. Before the blanket night. Before any of your own trauma. Your baseline is already: *Danger is normal. Don't relax. The other shoe will drop.*

**Step 6:** Draw a loop arrow from the fifth box back to the second. Label it **"GHOSTS KEEP PASSING"**

And the cycle continues. Each generation adds more weight. More loss. More alarms. The pile gets heavier.

Now point to the loop and say:

This is why you feel foreboding for no reason. This is why calm feels like a setup. This is why you're always waiting for the next bad thing.

**That's not paranoia. That's inheritance. That's ghosts you never met living in your nervous system.**

Good. That's grounding. You're here. Not there. Reset complete.

### [63:00 – 70:00] THE MIRROR (Silent – No talking)

You don't have to say a word. Just sit. Breathe. And notice.

If you've ever felt anxious when nothing was wrong.

If you've ever woken up already triggered – before you even had a thought.

If you see the same patterns in your family – the same fights, the same divorces, the same addiction, the same rage – generation after generation.

If you feel like you were born tired.

If calm feels dangerous. If peace makes you suspicious.

If you've ever been told you're "just like your father" – and it wasn't a compliment.

If you've ever looked at your kids and seen the same fear in their eyes that you had in yours.

If you've ever felt like you're carrying something heavy – and you don't know where it came from.

That's not your fault. You didn't start the fire. You just got handed the match.

That's the mirror. That's the machine showing you who wired it before you ever took a breath.

---

### [70:00 – 75:00] THE SHIFT + CLIFFHANGER

Here's your tool for this week. One thing.

When the foreboding hits – when you feel that dread that doesn't have a reason – don't fight it. Don't call yourself crazy. Don't try to logic your way out of it.

Just name it. Say it out loud or in your head:

**"That's not mine. That's a ghost."**

That's it. You're not trying to banish it. You're just separating your life from what you inherited. Naming it gives your brain one second of air. One second to ask: *Is this my alarm, or is this someone else's?*

You're free. See you next session.
